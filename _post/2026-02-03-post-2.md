My Struggle with SQL and How I 
Improved
Among all the subjects during my first year of Computer Engineering, Database Systems was 
probably the most challenging for me.
The theory behind databases appeared simple at first. Tables, rows, columns, and 
relationships sounded straightforward during lectures. The real challenge began when I had to 
implement SQL queries myself.
MySQL quickly became my biggest weakness.Simple SELECT statements were manageable, 
but joins, nested queries, grouping operations, and normalization concepts required a 
completely different style of thinking compared to programming.Many times my query 
would execute successfully but return incorrect results. Finding logical mistakes inside SQL 
queries often took longer than writing the query itself.
#MLwithDrBilalAhmad #DrBilalAhmad #MLProject
Dr. Bilal Ahmad frequently reminded us that database skills cannot be developed through 
theory alone. SQL is a practical skill that improves only through repetition and 
experimentation.Eventually, I started practicing smaller queries every day instead of waiting 
for assignments or exams. Slowly, concepts that once seemed confusing started becoming 
familiar.
The biggest lesson I learned was that struggling with a subject does not mean you cannot 
master it. Sometimes improvement simply requires more practice than expected.
